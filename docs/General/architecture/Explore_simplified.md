Here is the Simplified PRD for the fist Stage, Hot Takes Feed: 

# Curios Explore v1 — Simplified PRD

“Hot Takes Feed”

⸻

1. Product Direction

We simplify the experience radically.

No graph.
No rabbit holes.
No battle cards.
No complex exploration UI.

The entire experience focuses on ONE thing:

Turning news into provocative AI-generated insights people want to share.

This becomes the foundation layer for future evolution.

⸻

2. Core Product Insight

People do not share news.

People share:

* interpretations,
* predictions,
* controversial implications,
* smart-sounding takes.

Therefore:

Curios Explore should not look like a news app.

It should feel like:

“an AI that sees the second-order consequences of everything.”

⸻

3. Product Goal

Create a highly-scrollable, visually modern feed where every item contains:

* a strong AI-generated hot take,
* emotional tension,
* future implications,
* a clean shareable format.

Think:

* TikTok pacing
* Apple-level visual polish
* AI-native intelligence layer

⸻

4. Simplified Experience

⸻

FEED ONLY

The entire v1 is a single immersive feed.

Each card contains:

1. Background visual
2. Main headline
3. AI-generated hot take
4. Optional quick expansion
5. Share CTA

That’s it.

⸻

5. Feed Card Structure

Layout

FULLSCREEN / EDGE-TO-EDGE CARD

⸻

Card Anatomy

1. Background Visual

NOT:

* generic news thumbnails
* random article images

Instead:

* cinematic visual
* abstract AI-generated imagery
* futuristic editorial aesthetic

Generated using:

* GPT Image-2
* Nano Banana
* or stylized transforms of article images

⸻

2. Headline

Minimal.
Large typography.
2–3 lines max.

Example:

Meta launches AI glasses

⸻

3. Hot Take (CORE FEATURE)

Main focal point.

Example:

“The smartphone era is ending faster than people realize.”

This is the product.

⸻

4. Optional Expand

Tap to reveal:

* “Why this matters”
* “What happens next”
* “Who wins”
* “Who loses”

Keep this lightweight.

NO deep navigation yet.

⸻

5. Share Button

Critical.

Generate:

* beautiful story-style card,
* screenshot-ready layout,
* animated export later.

⸻

6. AI Content Generation

⸻

INPUT

News article:

* title
* snippet
* source
* optional image

⸻

OUTPUT

Generate:

Required

* Hot Take

Optional Secondary Fields

* Why this matters
* Prediction
* Startup opportunity
* Meme line

⸻

Example

INPUT:

Google releases AI coding agent

OUTPUT:

Hot Take:
“Junior developer jobs are disappearing before universities can adapt.”
Why this matters:
“Software teams may shrink dramatically.”
Prediction:
“Solo billion-dollar startups become normal.”

⸻

7. Tone Guidelines

The AI voice should feel:

* intelligent,
* concise,
* emotionally charged,
* slightly provocative,
* futurist,
* socially shareable.

⸻

Avoid

* clickbait trash,
* cringe Twitter energy,
* doomposting overload,
* generic summaries.

⸻

Desired Feel

Like:

* a smart VC,
* a sci-fi strategist,
* a founder predicting the future.

⸻

8. Feed Ranking

Replace:

latest news

with:

most interesting implications

⸻

Curiosity Score

Rank feed items by:

* future impact
* controversy
* novelty
* shareability
* emotional reaction
* startup relevance

⸻

Example Formula

score =
(shareability * .3)
+ (future_impact * .25)
+ (novelty * .2)
+ (controversy * .15)
+ (founder_relevance * .1)

⸻

9. Visual Direction

⸻

Design Principles

The feed should feel:

* cinematic,
* fluid,
* premium,
* AI-native,
* alive.

⸻

Inspiration

* Apple Invites
* Cosmos
* Linear
* Nothing OS
* high-end sci-fi UI

⸻

Rules

DO

* oversized typography,
* full-bleed imagery,
* subtle motion,
* depth blur,
* gradient overlays,
* minimal chrome.

⸻

DO NOT

* RSS grids,
* tiny thumbnails,
* boxed cards,
* cluttered metadata,
* “news portal” aesthetics.

⸻

10. Motion Design

Subtle but important.

Use:

* parallax,
* fade depth,
* kinetic scroll,
* smooth expand transitions.

Avoid:

* flashy animations,
* crypto-app vibes,
* noisy motion.

⸻

11. Sharing System

Sharing is core growth infrastructure.

Every card should export beautifully.

⸻

Share Output

Auto-generate:

* Instagram-story style image
* vertical card
* dark/light variants
* animated version later

⸻

Goal

Users should want to post:

“Look at this insane AI take.”

NOT:

“Here’s an article.”

⸻

12. Technical Architecture

⸻

Pipeline

Serper News API
→ dedupe
→ article enrichment
→ LLM hot take generation
→ curiosity scoring
→ image generation/stylization
→ feed ranking
→ client delivery

⸻

AI Layer

Use:

* GPT-5.5 / reasoning model
* lightweight fast model for ranking
* GPT Image-2 / Nano Banana for visuals

⸻

13. Performance Requirements

The app must feel instant.

Targets:

* feed render < 1s
* next card preload
* async image streaming
* smooth 60fps scroll

⸻

14. MVP Scope

Included

✅ Hot takes
✅ AI-generated visuals
✅ Shareable cards
✅ Curiosity ranking
✅ Feed motion polish

⸻

Excluded (for now)

❌ Rabbit hole graphs
❌ Battle cards
❌ Debate systems
❌ Deep exploration
❌ Social comments
❌ Complex navigation

⸻

15. Future Evolution Path

The architecture should allow gradual evolution later:

V2:

* lightweight rabbit holes

V3:

* battle cards

V4:

* explorable graph intelligence

But v1 wins by:

being simple, fast, elegant, and addictive.

⸻

16. Final Product Positioning

Curios Explore is not:

“AI news.”

It is:

“An AI-generated feed of the internet’s most important implications.”
